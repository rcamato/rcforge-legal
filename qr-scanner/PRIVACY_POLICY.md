# RCForge QR Scanner – Private & Offline

## Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** July 19, 2026
**Developer:** Rodzil Camato
**Support email:** [rodzilcamato@gmail.com](mailto:rodzilcamato@gmail.com)

This Privacy Policy explains how **RCForge QR Scanner – Private & Offline** (“RCForge,” “the app,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) handles information when you use the app.

RCForge is designed as a privacy-focused, offline-first QR scanner and generator. Its core scanning, generation, history, and Collections features are designed to operate locally on your device without requiring an account or an RCForge-operated cloud service.

## 1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to the RCForge mobile application and its related features.

It does not apply to third-party websites, applications, advertising services, payment platforms, cloud-storage services, or other external services that you choose to open or use through RCForge.

Those third parties are governed by their own privacy policies and terms.

## 2. No account or RCForge cloud history

RCForge does not currently require you to:

* Create an account.
* Sign in.
* Provide a profile.
* Upload your QR history to an RCForge server.
* Synchronize your Collections through an RCForge cloud service.

RCForge does not currently operate a remote account database or cloud history service for scanned or generated QR records.

## 3. Information stored locally

RCForge may store the following information locally on your device:

* Scanned QR contents.
* Generated QR contents.
* Custom record names.
* Favorites.
* Detected QR types.
* Scan and generation dates.
* QR visual-style settings.
* Foreground and background colors.
* Gradient configuration.
* Logos selected by the user.
* Module, eye, and frame styles.
* RCForge branding settings.
* Protected QR information.
* Scanner preferences.
* Theme preferences.
* Usage counters needed to enforce free or premium feature limits.
* Import and export metadata.

QR records and Collections are primarily stored locally using SQLite.

Settings and preferences may be stored using local application preferences or other app-private storage.

## 4. Camera access

RCForge requests access to your device camera so it can scan QR codes.

Camera images are processed on the device for QR detection. RCForge does not intentionally upload camera frames or scanned QR contents to an RCForge server.

Camera access is used only while scanning features are active, subject to the permissions and controls provided by your device.

You may deny or revoke camera access through your device settings, but camera scanning will not work without it.

## 5. Images, logos, and files

RCForge may allow you to select images and files for purposes including:

* Scanning a QR code from an image.
* Selecting a logo for a generated QR code.
* Importing Collections from JSON or CSV.
* Exporting Collections to JSON or CSV.
* Saving generated QR images.
* Sharing generated QR codes or record contents.

RCForge accesses only the images or files that you select through the available system picker or supported app workflow.

Selected images, logos, and files are generally processed locally.

RCForge does not intentionally upload selected images, imported files, logos, or exported files to an RCForge server.

## 6. Private Mode

Private Mode is designed to prevent newly scanned QR results from being added to the local Collections database.

Private Mode does not prevent information from being:

* Displayed on your screen.
* Copied to the clipboard.
* Opened in another application.
* Shared or exported by you.
* Captured in a screenshot.
* Processed by an external application after you select an action.

You remain responsible for protecting information that you copy, open, export, or share while Private Mode is active.

## 7. User-controlled actions

RCForge allows you to choose when to:

* Open a website.
* Initiate a phone call.
* Compose an email.
* Compose an SMS message.
* View Wi-Fi information.
* View contact information.
* Copy content.
* Save an image.
* Import or export data.
* Share content with another application.

When you select one of these actions, the receiving browser, phone application, email service, messaging application, gallery, file service, cloud provider, or other external application may receive the relevant information.

External services handle that information according to their own privacy policies and practices.

The free version of RCForge may display Google AdMob banner advertisements on appropriate non-camera screens.

Google AdMob is a third-party advertising service operated by Google.

Depending on your location, privacy choices, device configuration, application configuration, and Google’s services, the Google Mobile Ads SDK may process information including:

* IP address.
* Approximate location inferred from IP address.
* Application interactions.
* Advertisement interactions.
* Diagnostic and performance information.
* Device identifiers.
* Advertising identifiers.
* App-set identifiers.
* Account-related identifiers where applicable.
* Information used for advertising, measurement, analytics, security, and fraud prevention.

RCForge does not intentionally provide the following information to Google AdMob for advertising purposes:

* Scanned QR contents.
* Generated QR contents.
* Local Collections.
* Custom record names.
* Wi-Fi passwords or credentials contained in QR codes.
* Contact information contained in QR codes.
* Imported JSON or CSV contents.
* Exported files.
* Uploaded logos.
* Protected QR payloads.

RCForge does not use QR contents to select or personalize advertisements.

## 11. Analytics and crash reporting

RCForge does not currently use an RCForge-operated network analytics or crash-reporting service.

The app may keep limited local counters, preferences, or diagnostic state needed for app functionality. These local values are not intentionally transmitted to an RCForge server.

Google AdMob may process interaction and diagnostic information as described in the Advertising section.

If RCForge later adds a network-based analytics or crash-reporting service, this Privacy Policy and the Google Play Data Safety declaration will be updated before that service is activated.

## 12. Protected QR

Protected QR features may use local cryptographic operations to encode, sign, decode, or verify supported QR information.

Protected QR processing is designed to occur locally.

Protected QR does not provide official identity verification and does not guarantee:

* Authorship.
* Ownership.
* Accuracy of the underlying information.
* Confidentiality.
* Absolute security.
* Authorization of the person presenting the QR code.

Users should not place highly sensitive information into a QR code unless they understand the risks of displaying, exporting, printing, or sharing that code.

## 13. Data retention

Local QR records and Collections remain on your device until you:

* Delete them.
* Clear the app’s data.
* Reset the device.
* Uninstall the app.
* Replace or lose the device.
* Experience storage failure or corruption.

Uninstalling RCForge normally removes app-private local data, subject to your device’s operating system and backup settings.

Device manufacturers, operating-system providers, or account-backup services may separately back up application data according to your device and account configuration.

RCForge does not control those backup services.

## 14. Data deletion

You can delete local information through the available RCForge record-management controls.

You may also remove locally stored app data by:

* Clearing RCForge storage through Android settings.
* Uninstalling the app.

Files previously exported, copied, uploaded, printed, saved, or shared must be deleted separately from their destination.

Because RCForge does not currently operate a user-account or cloud-history service, RCForge generally cannot remotely access or delete QR information stored only on your device.
